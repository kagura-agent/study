Key Finding

1. Dynamic Retrieval Thresholds (Quarq Argus Pattern)

Applied intent-aware match thresholds to wiki search, replacing a fixed 60% cutoff:

  • Historical queries (aggregation/timeline): 40% — broader recall captures more related context
  • Current/point-fact queries: 80% — strict precision avoids noise
  • Default (recent/neutral): 60% — balanced baseline

Result: search-bench.sh maintained 100% accuracy (10/10 queries, 17/17 items) while allowing intent-appropriate flexibility.

Insight: One-size-fits-all thresholds force a precision-recall tradeoff at design time. Intent classification at query time lets you have both — tight when you need answers, loose when you need context.

Operational Lessons (from night work)

2. Biome Format Drift on Rebase

NemoClaw CI's static-checks hook modifies files post-commit. After rebasing onto an advanced upstream, always run npx biome format --write on changed files before pushing.

3. "Files modified by hooks" = Check Upstream First

When CI shows this error after a rebase, the likely cause is upstream advancing past your rebase point — not a local formatting issue. A simple re-rebase often fixes it without manual formatting.
